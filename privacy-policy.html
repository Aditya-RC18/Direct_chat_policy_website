Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

Direct Chat is built to open WhatsApp conversations quickly, without asking you to save a new contact first. This page explains what information the app handles and how.

What Direct Chat does

Direct Chat lets you open a chat on WhatsApp or WhatsApp Business by typing or scanning a phone number, without needing to save that number as a contact on your phone.

Data stored on your device

To make the app useful, it keeps some information locally on your phone, including:

  • Your chat history (numbers you've messaged, nicknames you've set, and message previews)
  • Favorites you've saved
  • Message templates you've created
  • Groups you've created and their members
  • App preferences, such as theme and default country code

This data stays on your device unless you choose to enable sync (see below).

Optional sync

If you turn on device sync/pairing, the same data listed above is copied to a private, per-device storage space so it can be restored on another device you pair. We do not read, share, sell, or use this data for advertising. It exists only so your own data can follow you across your own devices.

What we don't do

  • We don't read your WhatsApp messages — Direct Chat only opens WhatsApp with a pre-filled number and message; the conversation itself happens inside WhatsApp, outside our app.
  • We don't sell or share your data with advertisers or third parties.
  • We don't require you to create an account or log in.

Permissions the app may ask for

  • Camera — only used if you choose to scan a QR code or take a photo for a group.
  • Internet — used only for optional sync between your own devices.

Deleting your data

Since Direct Chat doesn't use accounts, you can clear your data at any time directly from within the app. See our Manage Your Data page for the exact steps.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we'll update the date at the top of this page.
